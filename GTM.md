# Go-To-Market Strategy (GTM)

## Target User Persona
- **Who**: Engineering Managers, CTOs, or Lead Developers at Series A/B startups.
- **Pain Point**: Team size grew from 5 to 25. Everyone expensed different AI tools on their corporate cards. They are now spending $2,000+/mo on redundant AI subscriptions without a centralized policy.
- **Where they hang out**: Hacker News, Twitter/X (Tech Twitter), specialized Slack/Discord communities (CTO Craft, Rands Leadership).

## The First 100 Users Plan
**Week 1: Founder Outreach**
- DM 50 founders/CTOs in our immediate network offering a free, manual audit.
- Post a "Show HN" on Hacker News with a catchy title: "Show HN: We built a tool that tells you exactly how much your team is overspending on AI."

**Week 2: Content Marketing**
- Publish a data-backed blog post: "The Average Startup Wastes $400/mo on Redundant AI Tools."
- Distribute in CTO Slack channels.

**Week 3: The "Unfair Advantage"**
- Leverage Credex. Credex already has a waitlist of startups looking for infrastructure credits.
- Send an email blast to the Credex waitlist: "Before we get you discounts on AWS, let's optimize your current AI SaaS spend for free."

## Unfair Distribution Channel
The deep integration with Credex is the unfair advantage. SpendLens acts as a top-of-funnel lead generator for Credex's core business (negotiating volume discounts). In return, Credex provides distribution to its existing audience of cost-conscious startup founders.
